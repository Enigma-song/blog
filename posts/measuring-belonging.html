Measuring Belonging: A Youth Index for Social Mobility

March 14, 2026 · 4 min read

Many mobility dashboards measure income and education, but they overlook the softer signals that keep young people anchored to a community. A youth belonging index would combine data on housing stability, civic participation, and school engagement.

1. Combine economic and civic metrics

Employment and wage growth should sit alongside volunteerism and local participation rates to capture full inclusion.

2. Track lived experience

Short surveys can capture perceived safety, mentorship access, and feelings of future opportunity.

3. Make the data usable

Dashboards should be built for schools and youth councils, not just economists.

  2. UNICEF. "Innocenti Report Card." Use for child and youth well-being indicators.
