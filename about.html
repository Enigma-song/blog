About the Author

I'm a Grade 11 student with a deep interest in economics and social science, and a long-term dream of studying at Harvard. I write to sharpen my thinking, cite what I learn, and explore how policy can create dignity and opportunity.

This blog is a personal research journal. Each essay begins with a question, and every claim is linked to a cited source or dataset. My focus sits at the intersection of three themes: the future of work and migration, climate-resilient cities, and the measurement of social mobility.

I believe thoughtful policy can be both ambitious and humane. My goal is to explore big ideas with a student's curiosity and the rigor of academic sources.

Want to collaborate, suggest a source, or ask a question? Email me at hello@example.com.
